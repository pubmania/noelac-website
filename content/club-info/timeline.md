The club timeline

> [!NOTE]
> We are currently populating our historical archives. Check back soon for more "Lhasa Lore."

{{< timeline >}}

{{< timelineItem icon="graduation-cap" header="The Foundation" badge="1991 - 1992" >}}
NOELAC was formed at an inaugural meeting on <strong>August 1st, 1991</strong>. The club received official Kennel Club approval in July 1992, establishing our roots in the North of England.
{{< /timelineItem >}}

{{< timelineItem icon="sun" header="The First Show" badge="1993" >}}
The club's first Open Show was held on <strong>May 30th</strong>. To meet our commitment to the region, we began holding two Open Shows per year—one to the East and one to the West of the Pennines.
{{< /timelineItem >}}

{{< timelineItem icon="star" header="Championship Status" badge="2001" >}}
A major milestone: we held our very first <strong>Championship Show</strong>. It was judged by <strong>Mrs. Tina Lewis</strong>, who served as the club's first Chairperson and was instrumental in our formation.
{{< /timelineItem >}}

{{< timelineItem icon="eye" header="Commitment to Health" badge="2001 - Present" >}}
The club took an active role in overseeing breed well-being, specifically contributing financially to research into <strong>Progressive Retinal Atrophy (PRA)</strong> to help protect the sight of future generations.
{{< /timelineItem >}}

{{< timelineItem icon="heart" header="Annual CC Status & Patronage" badge="2009" >}}
A landmark year: NOELAC was granted <strong>annual Championship Certificates (CCs)</strong>. We were also proud to appoint <strong>Mrs. Tina Lewis</strong> (Ragoosa Kennels) as our official Club Patron.
{{< /timelineItem >}}

{{< timelineItem icon="shield" header="The Modern Era" badge="Present Day" >}}
Today, the club continues to uphold the <strong>RKC Code of Ethics</strong>, manages a dedicated Rescue service, and supports members across the North of England in celebrating the "Lion Dog."
{{< /timelineItem >}}

{{< /timeline >}}
